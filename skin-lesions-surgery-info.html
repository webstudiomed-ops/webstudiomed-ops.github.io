Skin Lesions Surgery – Excision, Biopsy & Removal

Skin lesion surgery involves the removal or biopsy of abnormal skin growths such as moles, cysts, lipomas, warts, or suspicious lesions. These procedures are performed for both medical and aesthetic reasons. Whether a lesion is benign or requires further evaluation, surgical removal ensures safety, peace of mind, and improved appearance.

“The goal of skin lesion surgery is precision — removing abnormal tissue safely while preserving healthy skin and achieving the best cosmetic outcome.”

Common Reasons for Skin Lesion Removal

Patients often undergo lesion surgery for one or more of the following reasons:

  • Suspicious or changing moles that require biopsy
  • Benign lesions that cause irritation or discomfort
  • Cysts or lipomas that increase in size
  • Lesions in visible areas that affect aesthetics or confidence
  • Preventing potential malignant transformation

Types of Skin Lesion Procedures

Depending on the type and location of the lesion, procedures may include:

  • Excisional Surgery: Complete surgical removal of the lesion and surrounding tissue.
  • Biopsy: Removing a small portion or the entire lesion for laboratory analysis.
  • Cyst or Lipoma Removal: Removing deep-seated or encapsulated growths.
  • Shave Removal: Suitable for superficial lesions such as raised moles.

Aesthetic & Functional Benefits

Skin lesion surgery not only eliminates medical concerns but also improves skin appearance. Procedures are performed with meticulous detail to minimize scarring and achieve natural, smooth results.

When to Seek Medical Attention

Patients should consult a specialist if they notice:

  • Rapidly growing or changing moles
  • Lesions that bleed, itch, or become painful
  • New lesions appearing after sun exposure
  • Any lump beneath the skin that continues to enlarge
